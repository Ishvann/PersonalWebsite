Data on CS Graduate School Admissions

January 1, 2015, 12:46 AM PST

Introduction

When I applied to graduate school, I wanted to know my chances of getting in. Many people wonder the same thing when they apply. I compiled data from official, public sources detailing the scores, ranges, and percentiles of applicants and accepted students to several top computer science schools. Using this data, hopefully you can gain a better understanding of where you stand and what is required to meet your goals.

Jan 1, 2015: This is still a work in progress and certain sections are incomplete.

Outline

Although this post refers to the computer science admissions processes, it may be relevant for similar fields such as electrical engineering and computaitonal science. The rest of this post are organized into several sections:
  1. Schools & Data Sources
  2. Number of Students
  3. GRE Scores
  4. GPA
  5. Funding
Other important components of the application such as your letters of recommendation and your statement of purpose are not discussed. For guidance on those, refer to other sources online.

GRE Scores

Before we look at data on a per-school basis, it is important to know how many students score X on each section. It turns out nearly 5% of computer science applicants and 6% of electrical engineers get a perfect 170 on the quant section. The plot below shows the distribution of GRE scores by major.
